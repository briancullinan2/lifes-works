Who is listening?

Jazz has attracted a wide and diverse audience over its one hundred year history. All races have both been performers and audiences for jazz. Caribbean, South American, Asian, Native American, Anglo, and most prominently African-American musicians have performed jazz and have been its audience.

At times racially and socially segregated, jazz has become the standard bearer for a distinctive 'American' music that is considered to be home-grown and a point of pride for the nation (Ward and Burns 2000:vii). We will discuss performers, audiences, venues and recording in more detail as we go along.
