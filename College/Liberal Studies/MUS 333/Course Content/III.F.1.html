Chick Corea

Another former member of Miles' bands (1968-70) is keyboard great Chick Corea who, along with Miles alumni Herbie Hancock and John Coltrane Quartet pianist McCoy Tyner, are probably the most influential post-1960s keyboard players in jazz. Read about Chick Corea.

Corea has created and continues to perform with some of the most important fusion groups working in jazz. Among many, including Circle (1969-72), is Return to Forever (1971-72) that created an interesting mix of jazz, Latin, and pop music. From 1973 into the late 1970s, an electric version of Return to Forever was fronted by Corea, now performing on Fender-Rhodes electric piano, Hohner Clavinet, Yamaha Organ, and Minimoog and ARP Odyssey synthesizers. The band also featured, in turn, on electric guitar Bill Connors, Earl Klugh, and virtuoso guitarist Al Di Meola, drummer Lenny White (another former Miles band member), and electric bass wonder Stanley Clarke. Read about Stanley Clarke.

"Vulcan Worlds" is a Stanley Clark composition that features unison ensemble playing that was heavily synthesized and full of electronics, all set over rock beats and funk bass lines. Listen to Return to Forever's "Vulcan Worlds."

In the late 1980s, Corea put together another virtuosic fusion group called the Elektric Band, which consisted of

Chick Corea, midi piano and synthesizers

Eric Marienthal, sax

Frank Gamble, guitar

John Patitucci, electric bass

Dave Weckl, drums

They released two very successful recordings, Elektric Band (1986) and Inside Out (1990) and further spawned an acoustic trio—Akoustic Band—of Corea (acoustic piano), John Patitucci (acoustic bass), and Dave Weckl (drums) drawn from the larger, electric ensemble.

"Inside Out" is the title track of the 1990 Eleckric Band release. It is a very commercially-appealing, fusion work. However, most of the chords used in the piece are major 7th chords and the piece also has a great deal of parallel motion. "Inside Out" opens with an interesting chord progression whose roots ascend in an alternating whole and half step pattern (am7/D-bm7/D-CM7/D-dm7/D-Eb = whole-half-whole- half steps), that is then followed by a root movement that descends byhalf steps (in mm. 10-11, we hear CM7-BM7-BbM7-A7-AbM7-Gm7).

Listen to Chick Corea and Elektric Band's "Inside Out" (1990).
