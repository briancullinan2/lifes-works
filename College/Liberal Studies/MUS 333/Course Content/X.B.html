Joseph ‘King’ Oliver

King Oliver was both the mentor of the young Louis Armstrong and one of the most important of all New Orleans Dixieland band leaders.

Read the article on Joseph ‘King’ Oliver

When King Oliver was at the peak of his popularity in 1918, he moved to Chicago. He eventually enticed Louis Armstrong to move north and join his band working and recording in the city. Most importantly, King Oliver’s group was the first African American ensemble to record a series of jazz pieces (forty-two sides in 1923 alone).
