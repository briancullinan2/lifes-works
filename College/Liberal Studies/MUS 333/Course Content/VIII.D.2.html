Duke Ellington

The Cotton Club’s ‘Jungle Music’

From 1927 to 1931 Ellington and his group were featured at the Cotton Club in Harlem where they played so-called ‘jungle music’ numbers that featured many faux African musical stereotypes: deep sounding drums, ‘growling’ brass effects (a specialty of trumpeter Bubber Miley), exotic-sounding harmonies, and melodies based on pentatonic (five pitch) and whole tone scales. Duke Ellington and his Famous Orchestra’s 1927 recording of “East St. Louis Toddle-Oo” exemplifies this then-popular style.

“East St. Louis Toddle-Oo” is roughly in A A B A form with new material added in a C section. This C section would be moved around by Ellington in recordings of other arrangements of this tune throughout the 1920s and it even disappears in recordings after 1937.

Listen especially for Bubber Miley’s ‘growl’ as you follow the chart below and listen to Duke Ellington and His Orchestra’s “East St. Louis Toddle-Oo” (1927).
