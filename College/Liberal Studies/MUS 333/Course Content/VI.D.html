Bebop and swing: rhythm sections

A similar gulf existed between bebop and swing when one looks at how the rhythm section accompanied soloists. Swing rhythm sections played static and predictable patterns, so that people could still dance while a soloist improvised. In contrast, bebop rhythm section accompaniment followed the ebb and flow of the soloistâ€™s improvisation to a very high degree. There was a sense of a dialogue between the soloist and the rhythm section. For example, swing pianists comped in swing or stride style that was regular and predictable, while bebop pianists injected commentary into the mix.

The swing drummer kept time on the high-hat cymbal which was reinforced by the snare and bass drums. The bebop drummer put the time in the suspended cymbal, while developing polyrhythmic layers on the other drums.

Overall, the differences between swing and bebop come down to the fact that swing was familiar, popular, commercial music that was comfortable and accessible for audiences. It was dance music. Bebop was much more agitated and consciously intellectual music.
