New York Swing

Introduction

In the late 1920s, New York City became the jazz center of the United States. Louis Armstrong, Duke Ellington and many other jazz innovators lived and performed there. Music publishing companies and recording studios proliferated. ‘Tin Pan Alley’ churned out songs capable of greater harmonic sophistication and evolution. Radio networks were based in New York and broadcasted big band music around the country. Musicians migrated to the ‘Big Apple’ from all over the country including New Orleans and Chicago bringing with them their experience in not only jazz bands, but also brass and military bands, vaudeville bands, and dance bands. The result was a cross-fertilization between educated musicians, music readers, and non-music reading improvisers. The jazz music that was composed and played in this melting pot of styles came to be known as New York Swing.
