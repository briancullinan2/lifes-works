“Dippermouth Blues”

King Oliver’s Creole Jazz Band recorded “Dippermouth Blues” in 1923 in Richmond , Indian just outside of Chicago. The Creole Jazz Band was another all-star ensemble that featured incredible talent: both Oliver and Louis Armstrong on cornets, Honré Dutrey on trombone, Johnny Dodds on clarinet, Lil Hardin (soon to be Mrs. Armstrong) on piano, Bill Johnson on banjo and vocal break, and drummer Baby Dodds.

“Dippermouth Blues” is a 12 bar blues and features the dense, polyphonic style of Dixieland that was so characteristic of the style.
