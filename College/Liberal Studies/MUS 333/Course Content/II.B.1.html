Cut 'n' mix: early rap, hip-hop, and jazz fusion (1970s through the 1980s)

Introduction

Early hip-hop and jazz fusion (cut 'n' mix) as found in the 1970s through the 1980s is characterized by straight-ahead hip-hop, vamp driven grooves that feature scratches, synthesizers, drum machines, and a prominent bass. There may or may not berapping or voice over by a DJ. If present, often the DJ will be referencing the jazz artist who is a collaborator on the recording. A solo jazz improv line, if present, is frequently just layered into the mix.
