Duke Ellington

“Mood Indigo”

Moreso than his ‘jungle’ period works, it was the widespread popularity of his 1930 recording of “Mood Indigo” that made Ellington an international star. Originally recorded as “Dreamy Blues,” it was soon renamed “Mood Indigo.” It was based on the popular song written by Ellington and clarinetist Barney Bigard with words by Irving Mills.

In “Mood Indigo,” you hear solos by Joe ‘Tricky Sam’ Nanton on trombone, Ellington on piano, Artie Whetsol on trumpet, Barney Bigard on clarinet, and Nanton on trombone to close.

Ellington’s arrangement was breathtakingly original. He turned the traditional scoring in a big band on its head! What is usually found—clarinet played in the high register with trumpet sounding the melody accompanied by trombone in a lower register sounding a counter melody—is inverted so that in “Mood Indigo” we hear a muted trumpet on top along with a plunger-muted trombone in its high-register played in harmony with the trumpet, while the clarinet is sounding more than an octave below the trumpet and trombone.
