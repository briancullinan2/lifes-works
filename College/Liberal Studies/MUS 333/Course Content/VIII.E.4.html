Kansas City Swing

“Doggin’ Around”

“Doggin’ Around” was recorded by Count Basie and his Orchestra in 1938, just six years later than “Moten Swing,” but this band is much more sophisticated and virtuosic. You’ll also hear the legendary Basie rhythm section. The sound and feel that these four men created became and remains the Basie band’s signature. No other band of any size or period of jazz music elicits such instant recognition.

The melody of “Doggin’ Around” was written and arranged by Edgar Battle and tenor saxophonist Herschel Evans. It’s in a 32-bar A A B A song form. As you listen, notice that the B section has no written melody, only chords. The B section was always improvised. Listen carefully and you’ll hear that the background riffs played during the solos in choruses 2, 3 and 5 are different for each soloist. No background riffs are played in section B during any of the solos.

The 5th Chorus also features a solo by the great Lester Young. Notice how different his tenor sax solo sounds than Herschel Evans’ solo in the 2nd Chorus. They were as contrasting tenor sax players as one could find. Lester Young was this cool, restrained, and consummately elegant player sitting, unruffled, amid the explosion that was the Basie band. As discussed earlier in the course, Young would have a seminal influence on the development of Cool jazz.

Follow the chart below as you listen to Count Basie and His Orchestra’s “Doggin’ Around” (1938).

Introduction

0:00-0:07 Piano introduction (Basie)

 

1st Chorus

0:07-0:15 8mm A Saxes play a call riff followed by a growling brass response.

0:15-0:23 8mm A Repeat

0:23-0:30 8mm B Improvised alto sax solo (Earle Warren) – brass punches in background.

0:30-0:38 8mm A Repeat of first A section.

 

2nd Chorus

0:38-0:53 A (both times) Tenor sax solo (Herschel Evans) – light brass riffs in background. Evans plays with a full-bodied sax sound that contrasts sharply with the lighter sound used during Lester Young’s tenor sax solo that follows.

0:53-1:01 B Evan’s solo continues – brass drops out.

1:01-1:09 A Brass returns with background riff.

 

3rd Chorus

1:09-1:24 A (both times) Trumpet solo (Harry ‘Sweets’ Edison) – light sax riffs in background.

1:24-1:32 B Baritone sax solo (Jack Washington) accompanied by rhythm section only.

1:32-1:40 A Baritone sax solo continues.

 

4th Chorus

1:40-2:11 Basie solos on piano for entire chorus accompanied by rhythm section only.

 

5th Chorus

2:11-2:26 A (both times) Tenor sax solo (Lester Young) – brass riffs in background. Young’s sax sound in much lighter Herschel Evans’. Young seems to float over the band. His lighter sound and playing style was a major influence the ‘Cool’ school of playing.

2:26-2:34 B Young’s solo continues - brass drops out.

2:34-2:42 A Brass returns with background riff.

 

6th Chorus

2:42-2:49 8mm Drum solo (Jo Jones)

2:49 to end Brass call riff and sax response riff – new musical material to end.
