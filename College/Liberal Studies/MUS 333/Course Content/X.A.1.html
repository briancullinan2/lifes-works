Dixieland style

By the early twentieth century, the brass bands and orchestras of Storyville were all featuring the latest music played in the city’s own distinct style. Also called Dixieland by later musical marketers, the music had the following characteristics.

  • Dixieland’s tempo was moderate to moderately fast (remember that this was dance music) and very lively and exciting. Usually Dixieland pieces were in duple meter.
  • Dixieland is a quite distinct jazz style, in that the ensembles practiced group improvisation, not as individual soloists. It was very spontaneous and only limited by the chord progression. Musicians would at times try to outdo one another and at other times cooperated in an ensemble passage.
  • Dixieland used primarily three forms: 32-bar AABA song form, 12-bar instrumental blues, and four-strain Ragtime form.
