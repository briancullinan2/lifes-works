New York Swing

"Organ Grinder's Swing"

Based on a children’s song, Sy Oliver’s arrangement of “Organ Grinder’s Swing” is basically an AB form. I say ‘basically’ because during the trumpet solos, the B section repeats three times in a row before returning to the melody of the A section. The bass line played by the baritone sax during the A melody appears below the melody line in the B section.

Follow the chart below as you listen to Jimmie Lunceford Orchestra's "Organ Grinder's Swing" (1936).

1st chorus

0:00-0:17 8mm A Muted trumpet, trombone, clarinet and temple blocks, no rhythm section.

0:17-0:33 8mm B Melody played by growling (plunger mute) trumpet accompanied by rhythm section and bari sax playing a boogie-woogie bass line.

2nd chorus

0:33-0:48 8mm The key changes from Eb major to G major. The celeste plays melody accompanied by temple blocks only.

0:48-1:05 8mm Pianissimo (very soft) saxes play melody. Guitar solos.

1:05-1:12 4mm Chromatic interlude played by clarinet w/sax section.

3rd chorus (?)

1:12- 1:29 8mm B Trumpet solo (Paul Webster) no mute with sustained sax background.

1:29-1:45 8mm B Plunger mute trumpet solo (Sy Oliver) with sax background. Straight 4-to-the-bar rhythm section feel. Bass plays line played by the bari sax in B. Saxes play boogie style background.

1:45-2:02 8mm B Plunger mute trumpet solo continues with sax background.

4th chorus

Return to the key of Eb.

2:02-2:18 8mm A Rhythm section drops out and muted trumpet, trombone, clarinet with temple blocks.

2:18 to end B Melody played by growling (plunger mute) trumpet accompanied by rhythm section and bari sax playing a boogie-woogie bass line to end

The influence of Jimmie Lunceford and his music spread far and wide. The precision, swing, fine arrangements and showmanship helped to shape the styles of Glenn Miller, Tommy Dorsey and many others.
